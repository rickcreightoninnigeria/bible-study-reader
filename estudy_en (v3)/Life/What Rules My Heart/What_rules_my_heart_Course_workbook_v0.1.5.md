## 3. Surface Idols & Deep Idols

> Each deep idol — power, approval, comfort, or control —
> generates a different set of fears and a different set of hopes.
>
> — Tim Keller

> We cannot uproot surface idols without first addressing
> the deep idols that give them life.
>
> — Anon

| What do you think of the above quotations? (Do you like them or not, and why?) |
|---|
| |

The rest of these Bible studies will distinguish between 'surface idols' and 'deep idols.'[^1] Surface idols are the outward expressions of idolatry — our visible behaviours, and the things we can be seen to worship and value. Deep idols are the deeper, underlying motivations and desires of the heart that drive those behaviours. Surface idols take us to *what* we worship. Deep idols take us to *why* we worship those things.

| How does God's glory reveal the emptiness of idols? | |
|---|---|
| Jer 10:6-7 | |

### Surface Idols

Surface idols are tangible, concrete things in life that we can easily see people pursuing. This includes things like money, career, success, family, romantic love, or community. Surface idols are the 'vehicles' through which we try to satisfy our deeper desires. As always, these things we focus on are not (normally) inherently bad — if they were obviously bad, we would be much less tempted to worship them! But they become idols when they take the place of God in our lives. They become our gods when they become our ultimate sources of meaning or identity. For example:

### Deep Idols

Deep idols are the heart-level motivations that we seek to fulfil through surface idols. Deep idols represent the root issues behind our outward actions. The four deep idols that we will focus on in these studies are: **power, approval, comfort, and control/safety.**[^2]

Our next four studies will look at these four deep idols one by one, and then we will turn to see how these deep idols are expressed through surface idols.

Remember: Surface idols are 'what' we chase, and deep idols are 'why' we chase them. The deep idols operate at a heart level, shaping how we value our surface idols. Deep idols reflect where we seek our identity, purpose, and security outside of God.

# SECTION 2: Deep Idols

> Sin in our hearts affects our basic motivational drives so they become idolatrous, "deep idols." Some people are strongly motivated by a desire for influence and power, while others are more excited by approval and appreciation. Some want emotional and physical comfort more than anything else, while still others want security, the control of their environment. People with the deep idol of power do not mind being unpopular in order to gain influence. People who are most motivated by approval are the opposite — they will gladly lose power and control as long as everyone thinks well of them. Each deep idol — power, approval, comfort, or control — generates a different set of fears and a different set of hopes.
>
> — Tim Keller, *Counterfeit Gods*, ch. 3

### 'Active' versions of Deep Idols

Some deep idols are active. You go out and grab what you want. **Power** is about mastery over the actions of others and **Control** is about mastery over the actions of self. **Approval** is about controlling the feelings of others (towards self), and **Comfort** is about controlling the feelings that the self experiences.

The next studies will look at each one of these deep idols in turn:

- **Power.** You love to have power over others.
- **Approval.** You love to be accepted by others.
- **Control.** You love to have control over your environment.
- **Comfort.** You love to feel pleasure and comfort.

### 'Avoidant' versions of Deep Idols

These four 'active' deep idols also have 'avoidant' versions, where instead of grabbing something you are avoiding its opposite.

**Power/Autonomy.** Is it more that you love having power *over* others, or that you fear being over-powered *by* others? (Or is it a bit of both)? Do you want power *over* others or autonomy *from* others?

**Approval/Rejection.** Is it more that you love the approval of others, or that you fear being rejected by them? (Or is it a bit of both)?

**Control/Risk.** Is it more that you love having control over yourself, or that you fear taking a risk and being overwhelmed by chaos? (Or is it a bit of both)?

**Comfort/Pain.** Is it more that you love pursuing comfort and pleasure, or that you fear pain? (Or is it a bit of both)?

We will also look at each of these 'avoidant' deep idols:

- **Freedom from Power.** You just don't like others (or perhaps even God?!) having power over you. You want autonomy.
- **Freedom from Rejection.** You hate it when people explicitly reject or criticise you or disapprove of what you are doing.
- **Freedom from Risk.** You would rather do nothing than risk doing the wrong thing. Who knows what chaos might result!
- **Freedom from Pain.** You want to avoid pain or discomfort.

>
> — C.S. Lewis

|---|
| |

When comfort is an idol it will show up as a (disordered) desire for freedom from pain, stress, and unpleasantness of any kind. If you are in its grip, then you tend to avoid stress, and indulge in self-soothing behaviours, especially those where gratification is immediate.

| How did Lot's choice of immediate gratification lead to trouble? | |

| Gen 13:8-13; 19:1-38 | |

It is not that self-soothing behaviour is wrong; far from it. The problem is *idolatrous*, compulsive, impulsive self-soothing behaviour. You are willing to give up productive, God-given, God-honouring tasks to gain fleeting pleasures and distractions.

# SECTION 3: Surface Idols

If deep idols are the underlying motivations that drive our hearts — the 'why' of what we seek — the surface idols are the 'what'.

Surface idols are more tangible, concrete things in life. They are things that we can easily see people pursuing, such as money, career, success, family, romantic love, or community.

Surface idols are the 'vehicles' through which we seek to satisfy our deeper desires. Surface idols are not usually inherently bad — they are often good things. They become idols when they take the place of God in our hearts and become ultimate sources of meaning or identity. In fact, the best things make for the most dangerous idols.
