# Session 0 — Welcome: Your Story, God's Story

---

[type: text; subtype: intro]

You are welcome here.

Whatever it cost you to be in this room — whatever questions you are carrying, whatever fears are sitting quietly at the back of your mind — you have come to the right place.

This series is not a debate. It is not a test. It is not a place where you need to have everything figured out before you belong. It is a place to ask real questions, to read the Bible together, and to discover more of the God who has already been at work in your life.

Before we open the Bible, before we get into anything theological, we simply want to start here: **with you, and with welcome.**

## What This Series Is

---

[type: text; subtype: bridge]

Over the coming weeks we are going to work through ten sessions together. We will read the Bible, ask questions, and think carefully — but never rush. Here is a simple map of where we are going:

---

[type: callout; subtype: general; eyebrow: Series Overview; term: Knowing God Through Jesus — 10 Sessions]

**Session 0** — Welcome: Your Story, God's Story *(this session)*
**Session 1** — How Can We Know God? Trusting the Bible
**Session 2** — Who is God? The Personal God
**Session 3** — Who is Jesus? (Part 1) Son of God and Lord
**Session 4** — Who is Jesus? (Part 2) Understanding the Trinity
**Session 5** — What is the Problem? Sin and the Human Heart
**Session 6** — What Did Jesus Do? The Cross and the Resurrection
**Session 7** — How Are We Saved? Grace, Not Works
**Session 8** — What Does It Mean to Follow Jesus? Discipleship
**Session 9** — New Identity, New Family: Belonging in Christ
**Session 10** *(optional)* — Living with Confidence: Witness and Perseverance

---

[type: text; subtype: bridge]

Each session will follow the same pattern, so you will quickly know what to expect:

- We will begin with an **introduction** to the theme
- We will **read a Bible passage** together
- We will **explore it** through questions — there are no trick questions, and no marks for right answers
- We will finish with a **reflection** and a **prayer**

You do not need any prior knowledge. You do not need to have read the Bible before. All you need to bring is yourself.

## Some Things Worth Knowing

---

[type: callout; subtype: misunderstanding; eyebrow: Common Concern; term: "What if I ask a question that seems ignorant or offensive?"]

*Think about how you feel about asking questions in this group before tapping 'Find out'.*

**Answer:** There are no ignorant questions here. Many of the deepest and most important questions — about the Trinity, about whether the Bible has been corrupted, about how Jesus can be the Son of God — are questions that serious thinkers have wrestled with for centuries. If you are thinking it, ask it. Every question will be taken seriously and answered as honestly as we can.

---

[type: callout; subtype: misunderstanding; eyebrow: Common Concern; term: "What if I'm not sure I believe everything yet?"]

*Sit with that question for a moment before tapping 'Find out'.*

**Answer:** That is completely fine. This series is not a belief checklist. Faith grows. Doubt is not the opposite of faith — it is often part of the journey toward it. What matters is that you keep asking, keep reading, and keep showing up.

---

[type: text; subtype: bridge]

Before we go any further, there is something important to establish together. Some of you may be in situations where your faith in Jesus is not known to your family or community, or where it carries real risk. This group needs to be a safe space.

## Closing Prayer

---

[type: text; subtype: closing]

We are going to close this session with a simple prayer. If praying out loud is new or uncomfortable for you, that is completely fine — simply listen and make the words your own silently if you wish.

If your group leader is praying aloud, the prayer might go something like this:

> *Father God — we don't fully understand everything yet, and that's okay. What we know is that you have drawn each person in this room toward yourself, and we believe that is not an accident. Thank you for the journey that has brought us here. As we begin this series, give us open hearts and honest questions. Help us to know you — not just know about you. And let each of us find in you the belonging and security that nothing else can give.*
>
> *In Jesus' name, Amen.*
