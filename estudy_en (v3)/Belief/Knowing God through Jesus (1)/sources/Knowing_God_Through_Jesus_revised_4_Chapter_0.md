# Chapter 0 — Welcome: Your Story, God’s Story

---

[type: text; subtype: intro]

You are welcome here.

Whatever it cost you to reach this point — whatever questions you are carrying, whatever fears are at the back of your mind — you have come to the right place.

This series is not a debate. It is not a test. You do not need to have everything figured out before you belong. It is a place to ask honest questions, to read the Bible together, and to get to know the God who has already been at work in your life.

And it is about more than what you now believe. It is about who you now are. Those two things are connected — but they are not the same thing. Over the coming chapters we will be exploring both: the truths that Christianity holds, and the identity that comes with belonging to Christ. For many people, the second of those turns out to be the deeper journey.

Before we open the Bible, before we get into anything theological, we want to start with this: **with you, and with welcome.**

## Your Story

---

[type: text; subtype: bridge]

Everyone has a story. You did not arrive here by accident. Something — or <b>Someone</b> — has been drawing you. Before we do anything else, we want to make space for that.

Many people come to faith not in a calm moment, but in a difficult one — when life has raised questions that need answers, and old certainties have stopped being enough. It may have been a period of real struggle: questions that went unanswered, circumstances that fell apart, a search for something solid that nothing else could provide. If that is part of your story, you are not unusual. You are in good company.

You don’t need to share more than you are comfortable with. There is no pressure. But we do want to know something of who you are.

## Some Things Worth Knowing

---

[type: callout; subtype: general; eyebrow: A Note on How You Got Here; term: God meets people in different ways]

*Tap to read before you go any further.*

**Answer:** People come to faith by very different routes. Some arrive through careful reading, argument, and honest intellectual searching. Some through the life of a person they respected. Some through a moment of crisis when everything else failed. Some through a dream or an experience they can’t fully explain, that brought an unexpected sense of peace or direction.

None of these routes is more valid than the others. God is not limited to one way of reaching people. What matters is not the road that brought you here — it is that you have arrived. You won’t be asked to defend or explain your journey. This series will simply walk forward with you from wherever you are.

---

[type: callout; subtype: misunderstanding; eyebrow: Common Concern; term: "What if I ask a question that seems ignorant or offensive?"]

*Think about how you feel about asking questions before tapping ‘Find out’.*

**Answer:** There are no ignorant or foolish questions. Many of the deepest and most important questions — about the Trinity, about whether the Bible has been corrupted, about how Jesus can be the Son of God — are questions that serious thinkers have wrestled with for centuries. If you are thinking it, write it down, and find a time to ask it. You will get the most out of these studies if you also have a chance to ask extra questions face-to-face with a leader or in a group. Every question should be taken seriously and answered as honestly as possible.

---

[type: callout; subtype: misunderstanding; eyebrow: Common Concern; term: "What if I’m not sure I believe everything yet?"]

*Sit with that question for a moment before tapping ‘Find out’.*

**Answer:** That is completely fine. This series is not a belief checklist. Faith grows. Doubt is not the opposite of faith — it is often part of the journey toward it. What matters is that you keep asking, keep reading, and keep moving forward.

## Confidentiality and Safety

---

[type: text; subtype: bridge]

Before going any further, there is something important to establish if you are doing this series in a group with other people together. (And it is best to do it in a group if you can.) If you are in a group, some of you may be in situations where your faith in Jesus is not known to your family or community, or where it carries real risk. This group needs to be a safe space.

## Closing Prayer

---

[type: text; subtype: closing]

We are going to close this chapter with a simple prayer. If you are meeting in a group, and praying out loud is new or uncomfortable for you, that is completely fine — simply listen and make the words your own silently if you wish. If you are on your own you can pray out loud or silently, as you prefer.

> *Father God — we don’t fully understand everything yet, and that’s okay. What we know is that you have drawn each person in this room toward yourself, and we believe that is not an accident. Thank you for the journey that has brought us here — however long, however difficult, however surprising that journey has been. As we begin this series, give us open hearts and honest questions. Help us to know you — not just know about you. And let each of us find in you the belonging and security that nothing else can give.*
>
> *In Jesus’ name, Amen.*
